Valencian Paella

What is it?

Valencian Paella is the symbol and most internationally renowned dish of Valencia. It is the perfect way to gather friends and family together, and cooking it is a ritual. The guests usually stay in the kitchen and chat with the cook while they wait until everything is ready.

The main ingredientes and method are always the same, though there can be some slight variations depending on the season and the cook's personal preferences.

Ingredients

The most popular ingredients for Valencian Paella are the following:

  • 500g of Valencia Designation of Origin rice.
  • Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • Salt
  • 1 chopped rabbit
  • 1/2 chopped free-range chicken
  • 250g of flat green beans
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • Paprika
  • 3-4 grated ripe tomatoes
  • 150g of Garrofó (another type of bean)
  • Infused Saffron
  • 1 rosemary sprig (optional)
  • 4L of water

Steps

  1. Browning the Meat to Build the Flavor Base

    In the paella pan, heat a generous drizzle of extra virgin olive oil over medium heat. Once hot, add the chicken and rabbit. Let them brown slowly until they are deeply seared on all sides. This step is essential for the dish's depth of flavor.

    Do not stir constantly. Allow that golden crust to form, as it will later melt into the broth. This is one of the secrets to achieving a good paella.

  2. Sautéing the Green Beans and Preparing the Sofrito

    Once the meat is browned, add the 250g of green beans and sauté them with the meat for a couple of minutes. Once they have softened slightly, push everything toward the edges of the paella pan to clear a space in the center.

    In that central space, add the minced garlic and sweet paprika. Lower the heat to prevent them from burning, and then immediately stir in the grated tomatoes. Cook until the sofrito has reduced and developed a deep, dark color and a thick, rich consistency.

  3. Simmering the Broth to Integrate Flavors

    Once the tomato has fully reduced, add 2 liters of water and the garrofó beans. Mark the liquid level—using a magnet or a spoon—so you can later identify the perfect cooking point for the rice. Add 2 more liters of water and let everything simmer for about 20 minutes.

    During this process, the broth will gain body as it absorbs the flavors of the meat, vegetables, and sofrito. This is the ideal moment to adjust the salt if needed.

  4. Adding the Saffron Infusion at Just the Right Time

    Once the broth has reduced to the marked level, stir in the saffron infusion. This is the perfect moment for its flavor and color to meld without boiling away. You'll see the broth begin to take on that unmistakable golden hue.

  5. Cooking the Rice to Perfection

    Add the 500g of Valencia Designation of Origin short-grain rice in a cross shape and spread it evenly without stirring. Cook over high heat for the first 8 minutes, then lower the heat for the final 7 minutes. Do not stir at any point to allow the traditional "socarrat" to form.

    The rice should be dry, perfectly cooked, and have a slightly crispy bottom layer.

  6. Resting and Serving

    Once cooking is finished, remove the paella pan from the heat and cover it with a clean cloth for 5 to 10 minutes. This brief rest is key for the rice to finish settling, allowing the flavors to fully meld and the texture to reach its ideal point.

    Serve the paella directly in the pan, placing it in the center of the table to share. Garnish with lemon wedges if you like, and enjoy the inviting aroma of this traditional dish.
